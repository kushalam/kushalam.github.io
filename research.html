Research Projects

These are the research projects I worked on during my graduate studies that lead to various publications and/or tool development.

Modeling and Analysis of Complex Systems Design Process

This project studies the engineering design process for complex systems and presents an approach for its modeling and analysis. Though many studies on design processes exist, there is still a need to further incorporate considerations of the social, cultural, and political aspects during process design. Proposed modeling in this work models design teams as learning agents who adapt their behavior based on changing information.

Decision-centric Foundations for Modeling Complex Evolutionary Networks

In this project, we developed a random utility based discrete choice analysis method to model the evolution of complex endogenous networks as a result of the decision-making of its constituent autonomous nodes. The goal was to study how the decisions and interactions of these nodes affects network structure and ultimately its performance. We demonstrated the application of the framework to airlines' decision-making on route-selection problems as well as the evolution of the Internet network.

Fleet-level Environmental Evaluation Tool (FLEET)

In this project, we developed a Matlab based tool for measuring system-wide environmental impact of aviation. The tool uses a mixed integer programming approach to airline fleet allocation under various technological, economic, and policy scenarios. It includes different modules for aviation market demand growth, airline decisions on aircraft acquisition, and simulation of airline competition, modeled in a system dynamics-type approach.
