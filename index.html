I have a Ph.D. in Aeronautics and Astronautics from Purdue University. My research interests include aircraft design and optimization, complex systems design, air transportation systems, and systems engineering.

In the past, I have worked on the conceptual design and sizing of a regional commercial transport aircraft as well as on the design and sizing of an unmanned aerial vehicle for high altitude surveillance. At Purdue, I worked on assessment of new aircraft technologies on aviation's environmental impact. In addition, I have worked on study of the effects on network performance as a result of the preferences and consequent decisions of the autonomous nodes.

My Ph.D. dissertation focuses on modeling and analysis of the complex systems design process. The objective is to model the design teams' decision-making and to use these models to identify heuristics for complex systems design.
